What's in the sky? 🔭

The Sun — our star! About one million Earths could fit inside it.
Mercury — the smallest planet and the fastest: one year there lasts only 88 days.
Venus — the hottest planet, hiding under thick golden clouds.
Earth — our home, the blue planet! The Moon travels along with us.
Mars — the red planet. Its rocks are rusty, and it wears a white ice cap.
The asteroid belt — millions of space rocks tumbling between Mars and Jupiter.
Jupiter — the giant! Its Great Red Spot is a storm bigger than Earth. Its four big moons are Io, Europa, Ganymede and Callisto.
Saturn — its beautiful rings are made of billions of pieces of ice. The gap is called the Cassini division.
Uranus — it rolls around the Sun lying on its side, so its rings stand up tall.
Neptune — the windiest planet of all, far, far away.
Pluto — a little dwarf planet with a big heart shape on it.
